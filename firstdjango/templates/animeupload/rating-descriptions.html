Ratings Descriptions

nudity

the state of undress a character is in

none: there is no nudity in the show

underwear:character's underwear is exposed in the show

censored:character's are display as naked but are censored often by lighting or with props (i.e. Austin Powers style)

butt: a character's butt is displayed in the show

barbie smooth:characters can be seen naked but their genitals are not drawn in. (much like a ken or barbie doll)

outline:characters might not be seen naked but their is enough emphasis on the outlines of their clothes that they might as well have been.

nipple:nipples of female characters are displayed.

full frontal:complete nudity is displayed in the show.

intimacy

the extent of physical intimacy shown on-screen

none: there is no intimacy in the show

light kissing:a character will lightly kiss another (no tongue)

deep kissing/grope:character's will deeply kiss or grope one another (stuff generally not accepted as public displays of affection)

sex: characters will have sex

intent

the way intimate acts are portrayed

none: no intentions are displayed

unintentional:any intimate that happen are accidental or unintentional

mischievous: intimate acts are intentional but could be considered a "pranks" or with minor ill will (this is a subjective view)

extremely malicious: these acts would be very "rape-y" and considered sexual assault

violence

the violence displayed in a show

none: no violence in the show

cartoon:the violence is considered comical and has no lasting effect (think tom and jerry frying pan stuff)

blood: characters will bleed when hit or have bruises but no lasting damage

realistic: characters suffer sustainable damage and have realistic consequence (hospitalization and death)

extreme: characters suffer extremely graphic violence (torture and excessive maiming)

gore

amount of gore

none: no gore

low:some blood, a little bleeding

moderate: moderate bleeding, bruising, and swelling

high: lots of bleeding, quick shots of graphic images

extreme: long shots of graphic images

morbid images

amount of morbid images displayed

none: no morbid images

unmarred dead:dead bodies are shown however, they are not damaged (you only know they are not sleeping through context)

silhouetted dismemberment: dismemberment is inferred through silhouettes

dismemberment: dismemberment is shown

emotions

the emotional tax a show has

none: the show has no emotionally difficult moments

low there are a couple emotional parts to the show (a sad back-story or heartfelt speech)

moderate: some emotional parts but overall not a challenging show to watch (here is where is where Disney's Bambi or Lion King would sit)

high: this show was make to choke you up

extreme: you will probably only watch it once (no happy endings guaranteed)

profanity

what kind of language they use

none: no swearing

mild not something you would say in-front of grandma (sh*t, god d*mn)

censored: the show will have characters curse but will censor themselves

uncensored: f-bombs are fair game

moral ambiguity

the uncertainty of who are the "good guys" and "bad guys"

none: characters are inherently good or bad

low a show might take an episode to make the villain relatable or have the hero make do something unheroic

moderate: a good amount of time is spent making the villains relatable, or showing the heroes unheroicly but it is still hard to make a case against the heroes

high: a major aspect of the show is to not have moral high-grounds

extreme: actions of the heroes and villains can be strongly debated for moral correctness
